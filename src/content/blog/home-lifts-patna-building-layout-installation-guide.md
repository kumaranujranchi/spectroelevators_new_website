## Types of Home Lifts Available in Patna

Before planning installation, it is important to understand the common types of residential elevators available.

### 1. Hydraulic Home Lifts

Hydraulic elevators use fluid pressure systems for movement.

**Best For:**
- Low-rise homes
- Duplex villas
- Budget-friendly installations

**Advantages:**
- Smooth operation
- High load capacity
- Lower installation cost

**Limitations:**
- Requires more space
- Higher electricity consumption

### 2. MRL (Machine Room-Less) Lifts

MRL lifts do not require a separate machine room.

**Best For:**
- Modern homes
- Space-saving layouts
- Luxury villas

**Advantages:**
- Energy efficient
- Compact design
- Quiet operation

**Limitations:**
- Higher initial cost

Machine-room-less systems are now widely used in residential elevators because of their compact installation requirements.

### 3. Pneumatic Vacuum Lifts

These lifts use air pressure technology and are highly compact.

**Best For:**
- Small homes
- Retrofitting existing houses
- Minimal civil work

**Advantages:**
- No deep pit required
- Stylish appearance
- Fast installation

**Limitations:**
- Lower passenger capacity

### 4. Traction Home Elevators

Traction lifts use ropes and counterweight systems.

**Best For:**
- Multi-floor homes
- Premium residential projects

**Advantages:**
- Smooth ride quality
- Energy efficient
- Long lifespan

## Understanding Home Lift Building Layout Planning

Proper building layout planning is the most important part of home elevator installation.

If lift planning is ignored during construction, installation later may require expensive structural modifications.

### Key Areas to Plan Before Installation

#### 1. Lift Location
The first step is selecting the right location for the elevator.

Common placement options include:
- Near staircases
- Inside stairwell voids
- Adjacent to walls
- External glass shafts
- Central lobby areas

Modern residential elevators can fit into compact areas and stair voids efficiently.

#### 2. Shaft Space Requirements
The lift shaft is the vertical enclosure where the elevator moves.

Typical residential lift shaft requirements depend on capacity and lift type.

For compact home lifts, shaft sizes generally begin around:
- 4 ft × 4 ft
- 4 ft × 5 ft

Some compact lifts require even less space.

#### 3. Pit Depth
The pit is the space below the elevator floor level.

Typical residential lift pit depth:
- Around 100–150 mm for compact home lifts
- Larger systems may require deeper pits

Some pneumatic lifts are pitless.

#### 4. Overhead Clearance
Sufficient headroom above the top floor is necessary for safe operation.

Most residential elevators require:
- Around 2400–2500 mm overhead clearance

#### 5. Machine Room Requirements
Traditional hydraulic systems may require a separate machine room.

However, modern MRL lifts eliminate this need, saving space.

### Standard Home Lift Dimensions

Home lift dimensions vary depending on passenger capacity.

#### 2-Person Home Lift
Approximate dimensions:
- Cabin size: 900 mm × 900 mm
- Compact and space-efficient

#### 4-Person Home Lift
Approximate dimensions:
- Cabin size: 900 mm × 1000 mm
- Shaft size: 1300 mm × 1450 mm

#### 6-Person Home Lift
Approximate dimensions:
- Cabin size: 950 mm × 1300 mm
- Shaft size: 1500 mm × 1650 mm

### Minimum Space Required for Home Lifts

Most residential elevators require approximately:
- 20–25 square feet of installation space

Compact modern lifts may require even less depending on technology.

Some advanced compact systems can fit into spaces starting around 880 × 1365 mm.
