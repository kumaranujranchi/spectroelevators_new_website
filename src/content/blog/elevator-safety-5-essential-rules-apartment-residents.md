## Why Elevator Safety Matters

Modern elevators are equipped with advanced technologies such as emergency brakes, overload sensors, automatic rescue devices, and door safety systems. Despite these features, accidents can still occur when people ignore safety guidelines.

Children playing inside elevators, forcing lift doors open, overloading cabins, or using elevators during emergencies are some common causes of lift-related incidents.

Proper awareness and responsible usage can significantly reduce risks and ensure a safe experience for everyone in the building.

---

## Rule 1: Never Overload the Elevator

One of the most common elevator safety mistakes is exceeding the lift’s weight capacity.

Every elevator has a maximum load limit clearly displayed inside the cabin. Ignoring this limit can lead to:
- **Sudden stoppage**
- **Mechanical strain**
- **Door malfunction**
- **Reduced elevator lifespan**
- **Safety hazards**

Overloading is especially common during shifting, festivals, or when transporting furniture and heavy goods.

### Tips to Avoid Overloading
- Check the weight capacity before entering
- Wait for the next lift if the cabin feels crowded
- Avoid carrying excessive heavy items together
- Use service elevators for furniture and construction materials

Modern elevators include overload warning systems, but residents should still follow responsible practices.

## Rule 2: Keep Children Supervised Near Elevators

Children are naturally curious, and elevators often become a place of play inside apartment buildings. However, this can be dangerous.

Children should never:
- Play with elevator buttons
- Jump inside the cabin
- Block elevator doors
- Enter elevators alone without supervision
- Put hands between closing doors

Even though modern lifts have sensors, unsafe behavior can still cause accidents or sudden stoppages.

### Safety Tips for Parents
- Teach children proper elevator manners
- Hold young children’s hands while entering or exiting
- Explain emergency alarm buttons calmly
- Never allow kids to play near lift doors

Educating children about elevator safety from an early age can prevent serious accidents.

## Rule 3: Do Not Use Elevators During Fire Emergencies

This is one of the most important elevator safety rules.

During a fire emergency, elevators should NEVER be used.

**Why?**

Because electrical systems may fail, causing the elevator to stop between floors. Smoke can also enter the shaft, creating life-threatening situations.

Instead, residents should always use staircases during fire emergencies.

### What to Do During a Fire
- Use emergency exits and stairs
- Follow building evacuation instructions
- Avoid panic and overcrowding
- Help elderly residents safely evacuate

Modern residential elevators may include fire emergency systems, but stairs remain the safest option during fires.

## Rule 4: Stay Calm If the Elevator Stops Suddenly

Many people panic when an elevator stops unexpectedly between floors. However, modern elevators are designed with multiple safety mechanisms to protect passengers.

A sudden stop usually does NOT mean the elevator will fall.

### What You Should Do
- Stay calm
- Press the emergency alarm button
- Use the intercom or emergency phone
- Wait for trained technicians
- Do not attempt to force open doors

### What You Should NOT Do
- Do not jump inside the elevator
- Do not try to climb out
- Do not force the doors open

Most elevators have automatic rescue devices (ARD) that move the lift to the nearest floor during power failures.

## Rule 5: Avoid Blocking Elevator Doors

Elevator doors are equipped with sensors, but repeatedly forcing them open can damage the mechanism over time.

Many residents use hands, bags, or objects to stop doors from closing. While sensors may detect obstacles, unnecessary force can still lead to:
- **Sensor damage**
- **Door alignment issues**
- **Increased maintenance costs**
- **Sudden lift malfunction**

### Safe Practices
- Use the “Door Open” button instead
- Allow passengers to exit first
- Enter and exit quickly
- Keep luggage and objects away from door tracks

Simple habits can greatly improve elevator safety and extend the lifespan of the lift system.

## Additional Elevator Safety Tips for Apartment Residents

Besides the five major rules above, residents should also follow these important safety practices:

### Avoid Jumping Inside Elevators
Jumping can affect elevator balance systems and may trigger emergency stopping mechanisms.

### Report Unusual Noises Immediately
If you notice:
- Strange sounds
- Jerky movement
- Delayed doors
- Burning smell
- Sudden shaking

Report the issue to building management immediately.

### Follow Capacity Guidelines
Passenger limits exist for safety reasons. Ignoring them can damage the elevator system.

### Use Handrails When Available
Elderly residents should use support rails for additional stability while entering and exiting.
