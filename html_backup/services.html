Service 01

Elevator Installation Process

Installing a new elevator is a complex engineering task that requires precise structural alignment. Our installation process is divided into distinct, transparent phases to ensure safety and code compliance.

1

Structural Site Survey

We measure shaft width, pit depth, overhead clearance, and load capacities of your building's lift column.

2

CAD Blueprint Design

Engineers generate a custom layout drawing showing bracket points, machine location, and cable pathways.

3

Mechanical Assembly & Wiring

Anchoring guide rails, hanging traction motors, routing steel cables, and connecting digital control boxes.

4

Safety Verification Testing

A 45-point inspection checklist verifying emergency brakes, ARD power failure rescue, and weight balancing.

Service 03

Elevator Modernization & Repairs

Is your existing elevator over 10 years old? Is it noisy, slow, or constantly breaking down? Our modernization service upgrades your elevator machinery and electronics without requiring complete structural shaft rebuilding.

  • Traction Machine Upgrades: Replace loud geared machines with whisper-quiet, energy-saving permanent magnet gearless motors.
  • Microprocessor Controllers: Upgrade legacy relay cabinets to 32-bit digital loop controllers for faster floor responses.
  • Cabin Aesthetic Face-lift: Install modern stainless steel finishes, energy-saving LED panels, and modern buttons.
