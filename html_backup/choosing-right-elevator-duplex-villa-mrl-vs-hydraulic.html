Choosing the Right Elevator for Your Duplex Villa: MRL vs. Hydraulic

SE
By Spectro Editorial Team
May 24, 2026
8 Min Read

Modern duplex villas are no longer just about luxury interiors and smart home automation. Today, homeowners are also focusing on convenience, accessibility, and long-term property value. Installing a home elevator has become a practical necessity, especially for families with elderly members, children, or homeowners planning for future comfort.

However, one important question often creates confusion:

Which elevator is better for a duplex villa — MRL or Hydraulic?

Both technologies have their own advantages, and the right choice depends on your villa’s structure, budget, space availability, and usage requirements. In this detailed guide, we will compare MRL (Machine Room-Less) elevators and Hydraulic elevators to help you make the best decision for your home.

Understanding Home Elevators for Duplex Villas

Home elevators are specially designed compact lifts meant for residential properties like duplex houses, villas, bungalows, and private residences. Unlike commercial elevators, residential lifts focus more on:

Smooth & Quiet Operation

Minimal noise and vibration within residential halls.

Space-Saving Installation

Highly compact design with small shaft footprints.

Elegant Design

Custom glass cabins, wood finishes, and premium controls.

Energy & Safety Features

Highly power efficient with emergency rescue systems.

The two most common types of residential elevators in India are **MRL Elevators** and **Hydraulic Elevators**. Let's understand both in detail.

What is an MRL Elevator?

MRL stands for **Machine Room-Less Elevator**. As the name suggests, this type of lift does not require a separate machine room. The motor and control systems are installed inside the elevator shaft itself.

MRL elevators generally use traction technology (gearless permanent magnet motors) and are becoming increasingly popular in modern duplex villas because of their compact and stylish design.

Advantages of MRL Elevators

  • 1
    Space Saving Design One of the biggest benefits of MRL lifts is that they eliminate the need for a separate machine room. This saves valuable construction space in duplex homes. For urban villas where every square foot matters, MRL elevators are an ideal solution.
  • 2
    Energy Efficient MRL elevators consume less electricity compared to hydraulic lifts because they use advanced traction systems. Some studies suggest they can use significantly lower energy than traditional hydraulic systems. This helps homeowners reduce long-term electricity bills.
  • 3
    Smooth and Quiet Operation MRL elevators offer smoother acceleration and quieter movement, making them perfect for luxury duplex villas. The riding experience feels more premium and comfortable.
  • 4
    Modern Appearance Most MRL home lifts come with sleek cabins, glass finishes, automatic doors, and modern aesthetics that enhance the overall look of your villa.
  • 5
    Faster Installation Since there is no need to build a separate machine room, civil work becomes simpler and installation time reduces considerably.

Limitations of MRL Elevators

Despite their advantages, MRL lifts also have some drawbacks.

  • Higher Initial Cost: MRL elevators usually cost more upfront compared to hydraulic elevators.
  • Complex Maintenance: Since the machinery is integrated inside the shaft, maintenance and repairs may require specialized technicians.
  • Weight Limitations: Some compact MRL systems may have lower load capacities compared to hydraulic lifts.

What is a Hydraulic Elevator?

Hydraulic elevators use fluid pressure to move the elevator cabin. A hydraulic pump pushes liquid into a cylinder, which lifts the elevator upwards. Hydraulic lifts have been widely used in low-rise buildings and residential properties for decades.

Advantages of Hydraulic Elevators

  • 1
    Cost-Effective Installation Hydraulic elevators are generally more affordable to install compared to MRL elevators. This makes them a preferred option for budget-conscious homeowners.
  • 2
    Excellent for Low-Rise Villas Since duplex villas usually have only 2–4 floors, hydraulic elevators work very efficiently in such structures.
  • 3
    High Load Capacity Hydraulic lifts can carry heavier loads more comfortably, making them ideal for larger families or homes where bulky items need transportation.
  • 4
    Smooth Ride Quality Hydraulic systems are known for providing stable and jerk-free movement, which is especially useful for elderly users.
  • 5
    Easier Maintenance Hydraulic lift systems are relatively simple in construction and easier to service compared to modern MRL systems.

Limitations of Hydraulic Elevators

Despite their advantages, hydraulic lifts also have some drawbacks.

  • Requires More Space: Traditional hydraulic lifts often need a machine room, which increases space requirements.
  • Higher Power Consumption: Hydraulic elevators generally consume more energy, especially during upward travel.
  • Slower Speed: Hydraulic lifts are slower than traction-based MRL elevators.
  • Oil Maintenance: Hydraulic systems require oil management and periodic inspection to avoid leakage issues.

Which Elevator is Best for a Duplex Villa?

The answer depends entirely on your architectural structure, budget allocations, and visual preferences.

Choose an MRL Elevator If:

  • You want a premium & modern look
  • Space in the home is limited
  • Energy efficiency is high priority
  • You prefer near-silent operation
  • You are building a luxury duplex villa
  • You want to enhance property value

MRL elevators combine modern traction technology, premium aesthetics, and efficiency.

Choose a Hydraulic Elevator If:

  • You have a tighter initial budget
  • Your villa has only 2–3 floors
  • You need higher loading capacity
  • You prefer simpler, localized maintenance
  • Space is not a constraints issue

Hydraulic lifts remain an excellent, dependable option for affordability and stability.

Important Factors to Consider Before Installing a Home Lift

Before finalizing your layout and placing an order, ensure you consult with an engineering specialist on these key structural checkpoints:

1. Available Space

Check concrete shaft dimensions, pit depth (typically 300mm to 1000mm), and overhead clearance at the top floor. Proper measurements prevent alignment issues.

2. Power Consumption

Confirm if the lift requires single-phase or three-phase electrical input. Energy-efficient systems with VVVF drives reduce long-term running costs.

3. Safety Features (Must-Haves)

Never compromise on safety. Ensure your elevator is equipped with:

Emergency alarm
ARD (Auto Rescue Device)
Door safety sensor
Overload protection
Power backup integration
Telephone link

4. Maintenance Support & Brand Reputation

Select a certified manufacturer providing strong local after-sales service and comprehensive Annual Maintenance Contracts (AMC) in Patna to guarantee smooth breakdown operations.
